# Organisation Profile
## Aim High School Complex

## 1. Organisation Overview

Aim High School Complex is a private educational institution focused on providing quality basic and secondary education. The school operates multiple academic classes and manages a large number of students whose academic performance must be assessed, recorded, and communicated to parents periodically.

The institution relies heavily on internal administrative processes to manage student data, examination scores, and academic reporting. One of the most critical operational processes within the school is the preparation and generation of student report cards after examinations.

Currently, the grading and report generation process is handled using Microsoft Excel spreadsheets. Teachers manually enter student scores into spreadsheets, after which administrative staff consolidate the data, compute totals, determine rankings, and prepare final report sheets for printing and distribution to parents.

This manual and semi-digital process has been in use for several academic terms but has increasingly become inefficient as the number of students and classes grows.

---

## 2. Core Operations Relevant to the Study

The focus of this project is the **student grading and report generation process**, which involves the following activities:

1. Teachers record student scores in Excel spreadsheets.
2. Individual subject sheets are compiled for each class.
3. Administrative staff consolidate the sheets into a master report file.
4. Totals and averages are calculated using formulas or manual calculations.
5. Students are ranked manually within their class.
6. Final report sheets are generated and printed.
7. Printed reports are distributed to parents.

This process occurs after each examination period and involves several teachers and administrative staff.

---

## 3. Current System Used

The organisation currently uses a **spreadsheet-based grading system**, primarily relying on Microsoft Excel.

The Excel system is used to:

- Record student scores
- Calculate totals and averages
- Rank students within classes
- Generate printable report sheets

Although Excel provides formula capabilities, the system is largely dependent on **manual data entry and manual coordination among staff members**.

---

## 4. Key Stakeholders

The grading system involves several stakeholders within the school environment:

### Teachers
Teachers are responsible for entering student scores into the grading sheets. They are the primary data input providers.

### IT Department
The IT staff maintain the Excel templates used for grading and provide technical support when issues arise.

### Administrative Staff
Administrative personnel consolidate grading sheets from different classes and ensure reports are prepared for printing.

### School Management
The school leadership uses the final reports to evaluate student performance and monitor academic progress.

### Parents and Students
Parents receive the final printed reports to assess the academic performance of their children.

---

## 5. Observed Operational Challenges

Through interviews and discussions with the IT department and staff members, several operational issues were identified in the current system:

### File Duplication
Multiple copies of grading files are sometimes created, which leads to inconsistencies and confusion over which file contains the correct data.

### Version Conflicts
Different teachers may work on different versions of the same file, causing conflicts during consolidation.

### Manual Calculation Errors
Because some calculations are performed manually or formulas are incorrectly applied, errors in totals and averages occur frequently.

### Slow Consolidation Process
Combining multiple spreadsheets from different classes is time-consuming and requires significant manual effort.

### Lack of Audit Trail
The system does not track who edited or modified records, making it difficult to identify the source of errors.

### Data Loss Risk
Files stored locally or transferred between devices may be lost or corrupted.

### Lack of Real-Time Analytics
School management cannot easily analyze student performance trends or access consolidated data instantly.

### Manual Ranking Process
Student ranking is done manually, which is time-consuming and prone to mistakes.

### Delayed Report Generation
It typically takes up to **five days after exams** for final reports to be prepared and distributed to parents.

### Printing Bottlenecks
The printing process requires reports to be printed individually, often forcing staff to work late hours to complete the task.

---

## 6. Motivation for Process Improvement

Due to the increasing number of students and growing operational complexity, the current spreadsheet-based grading system is no longer efficient.

The school is therefore interested in exploring a **more reliable and efficient grading process** that can:

- Reduce manual errors
- Speed up report generation
- Improve data consistency
- Provide better tracking of changes
- Enable easier data analysis
- Reduce administrative workload

This project aims to analyze the current grading process and propose an improved **hybrid solution** that addresses these challenges while remaining practical for the organisation.

---

## 7. Scope of the Project

This project focuses specifically on the **grading and report generation process** used by Aim High School Complex.

The study will:

- Analyze the current grading process (AS-IS)
- Identify inefficiencies and pain points
- Propose an improved process model (TO-BE)
- Develop a business case for implementing the new system

The goal is to design a solution that improves efficiency while remaining realistic for the school's operational environment.
