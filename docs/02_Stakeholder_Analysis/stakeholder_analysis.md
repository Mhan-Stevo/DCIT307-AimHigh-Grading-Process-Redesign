# Stakeholder Analysis
## Aim High School Grading Process Redesign

## 1. Introduction

Stakeholder analysis is an important step in business process improvement projects. It helps identify individuals and groups who are involved in or affected by the process under study.

For the Aim High School grading system, several stakeholders interact with the grading and report generation process either directly or indirectly. Understanding their roles, responsibilities, influence, and expectations helps ensure that the redesigned process addresses the needs of all key participants.

This section identifies the key stakeholders and analyzes their level of influence and interest in the grading system.

---

## 2. Identified Stakeholders

The following stakeholders were identified during discussions with the school’s IT department and administrative staff.

### Teachers
Teachers are responsible for recording student scores for tests, assignments, and examinations. They input student marks into the grading spreadsheets and ensure the accuracy of the academic records.

### IT Department
The IT department supports the technical infrastructure used for grading. They maintain Excel templates, assist staff with technical issues, and ensure that grading files are properly stored and shared.

### Administrative Staff
Administrative personnel consolidate the grading sheets from multiple classes and prepare the final report sheets for printing and distribution.

### School Management
The headmaster and management team oversee the academic performance of students and rely on grading reports to evaluate educational outcomes.

### Printing Staff
Printing staff are responsible for producing the final physical report sheets that are distributed to students and parents.

### Parents and Students
Parents and students are the final recipients of the report cards and depend on them to monitor academic progress.

---

## 3. Stakeholder Roles and Responsibilities

| Stakeholder | Role in the Process | Key Responsibilities |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Teachers | Data Providers | Enter student scores and verify accuracy |
| IT Department | Technical Support | Maintain Excel templates and provide technical assistance |
| Administrative Staff | Data Consolidators | Combine class results and generate final reports |
| School Management | Decision Makers | Monitor academic performance and ensure reporting standards |
| Printing Staff | Output Production | Print report sheets for distribution |
| Parents & Students | End Users | Receive and review academic performance reports |

---

## 4. Power–Interest Analysis

Stakeholders can be categorized based on their **level of influence (power)** and **level of interest** in the grading system.

### High Power – High Interest
These stakeholders are critical to the success of the project and must be actively managed.

- School Management
- IT Department

### High Power – Low Interest
These stakeholders have authority but may not be involved in daily operations.

- School Administration

### Low Power – High Interest
These stakeholders are heavily involved in the process but have limited decision-making authority.

- Teachers
- Administrative Staff
- Printing Staff

### Low Power – Low Interest
These stakeholders are affected by the system but are not directly involved in managing it.

- Parents
- Students

## 6. RACI Responsibility Matrix

A RACI matrix clarifies who is responsible, accountable, consulted, and informed in each step of the grading process.

| Process Activity | Responsible | Accountable | Consulted | Informed |
|------------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|----------|
| Enter student scores | Teachers | School Management | IT Department | Admin Staff |
| Maintain grading templates | IT Department | School Management | Teachers | Admin Staff |
| Consolidate results | Administrative Staff | School Management | Teachers | IT Department |
| Generate report sheets | Administrative Staff | School Management | IT Department | Teachers |
| Print reports | Printing Staff | Administrative Staff | IT Department | Teachers |
| Distribute reports | Administration | School Management | Teachers | Parents & Students |

---

## 7. Key Observations

From the stakeholder analysis, several important observations were identified:

1. Teachers are the primary contributors of grading data, making accuracy and ease of data entry critical.
2. Administrative staff perform most of the consolidation work, which currently requires significant manual effort.
3. The IT department plays an important support role but lacks tools to enforce data integrity across spreadsheets.
4. School management depends on accurate and timely reports to evaluate student performance.
5. Parents and students rely on report cards to monitor academic progress, making timely report delivery important.

These insights will guide the redesign of the grading process in later sections of the project.

---

## 8. Conclusion

The stakeholder analysis highlights the various individuals and groups involved in the grading and reporting process at Aim High School Complex. Each stakeholder has different responsibilities and levels of influence within the system.

Understanding these relationships helps ensure that the proposed solution addresses operational challenges while meeting the needs of all participants involved in the grading process.

This analysis will support the development of the improved **TO-BE grading process model** in the next phase of the project.
